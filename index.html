State of the race

As of the most recent primary on April, 5 2016, Donald Trump is well in the lead of the Republican race. On the Democratic side, though, the gap between candidates is smaller. Currently, Hillary Clinton leads over Bernie Sanders with 1,302 to 1,121 pledged delegates.


Many news outlets insist that Bernie Sanders is out of the race, but this is not true. Sources say that "in order to make up ground, Sanders would need to win more than 70 percent of the remaining pledged delegates." In these comparisons, though, Hillary Clinton's lead is overestimated due to the inclusion of superdelegates. Superdelegates, unlike delegates, are mostly high-ranking Democratic party officials that are not bound to represent the popular vote. They may support any candidate for the nomination and change their vote at any time.


Based solely on the pledged delegate counts which represent American voters, Sanders needs to obtain about 57% of the delegates from the remaining 18 states to overtake Clinton. After his huge-margin victories in the latest caucuses (82% in Alaska, 70% in Hawaii, and 72% in Washington), this is not unachievable. Sanders brings momentum into the northern states where he has been expected to do his best.
